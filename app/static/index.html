meerato

A to-do list modeled on your life.

meerato groups your tasks the way you actually live, by the parts of your life they belong to. No deadlines to dread, just the right tasks surfacing on the right day, with the people who care kept in the loop.

What makes meerato different

Most to-do apps give you one big list and a pile of deadlines. meerato is built around how life is actually organized.

Buckets for every part of your life

Group tasks into the topics that belong together: Work, Private, your sports club, that other club, the kids' school. Each bucket keeps its world separate, so nothing bleeds together.

Statuses, not deadlines

A task isn't "due." It has a status that tells you when to act. Instead of a deadline, you set when the status should change, and the task lands on your daily to-do list right when it should.

Backlog Now Blocked Done

Built for collaboration

Share a link to any task so people are always up to date, or add their email and they'll be kept in the loop automatically as things change. No accounts to chase, no status meetings.

Built by nerds, for everyone

Power-user features under the hood, none of the complexity getting in your way.

Regex search

Search is a real regular-expression engine. Match by prefix, alternation or whole words (^Buy, invoice|receipt, \bbug\b) and combine it with status and bucket filters.

Simple, well-documented API

Create a task from anywhere with a single POST to your personal endpoint. Wire it into scripts, cron, shortcuts or your shell in seconds. Every parameter is documented right in the app.

Fully open source

The whole thing is open source as the meerkat project. Read the code, self-host it, or send a pull request. No lock-in, no black boxes.

 Smart add syntax

    

      
Type your task as usual. Add any of these and meerato sets them up automatically:

      

        
          

            #Bucket
            File it under an existing bucket by name, e.g. #Work or #Side project.
          

          

            @who@example.com
            Add a watcher by email (they get an invite). Add several by repeating it.
          

          

            !status
            Set the status: !now, !backlog, !blocked or !done.
          

          

            a date at the end
            Start the task in Backlog and automatically move it to
              Now on that day.
          

        
      


      
Date phrases (put them last)

      

        
          
tomorrowThe next day.

          
in 3 days · in 2 weeks · in 1 monthRelative to today.

          
next weekThe coming Monday (weeks start on Monday).

          
next month · next yearThe 1st of next month · January 1st.

          
monday … sundayThe next time that weekday comes around (also next friday).

          
on 2026-07-15A specific date (YYYY-MM-DD).

        
      


      

        
        Example: Email Sam about the report #Work @sam@example.com next week
      

      

        If an email isn't valid or a bucket name doesn't exist, that part stays in the
        task title untouched, nothing is lost.

 Searching tasks

    

      
Type to search across every task's title and notes. All conditions
        you give must match (filters and text are combined with AND).


      
Text is a regular expression

      
The free text (anything that isn't a key:value filter) is treated as a
        case-insensitive regular expression matched against the title and notes.

      

        
          
reportcontains "report" anywhere

          
^Buytitle/notes starting with "Buy"

          
invoice|receiptmatches "invoice" or "receipt"

          
\bbug\bthe whole word "bug"

        
      


      
Filters

      

        
          

            status:<value>
            Filter by status. Values: backlog (or open),
              on_list (or todo), blocked, done.
              Repeat to match any of several, e.g. status:blocked status:done.
          

          

            bucket:<name>
            Filter by bucket name (partial match), e.g. bucket:work.
              Use quotes for names with spaces: bucket:"side project".
          

        
      


      
Combine them

      
status:done bucket:work meeting: done tasks in a "work" bucket whose
        title or notes match meeting.
